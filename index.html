§ 03 — Field Notes

Insights.

Observations from the ground, not case studies dressed up as expertise.

Note 01 Behavior

The farmer always optimizes for the minimum viable action.

Every quest, every on-chain interaction, every Discord task — reduced to the lowest-cost repeatable unit. If your campaign can be scripted, it already is.

Note 02 Exploit

Points systems leak more than token drops.

Opaque points create asymmetric information. Teams think they've hidden the formula; farmers reverse-engineer it within 72 hours and own the leaderboard.

Note 03 Retention

Retention fails because the product was never the reward.

When the incentive ends, so does the behavior. Real retention requires reasons to return that exist independently of the drop.

Note 04 Detection

Wallet clustering alone catches the lazy ones.

Funding graphs, timing analysis, and behavioral entropy matter more than the obvious tells. Professional farms don't share gas wallets anymore.

Note 05 Design

Every anti-Sybil rule creates a new Sybil strategy.

Filters aren't walls — they're shape-changers. The question isn't whether farmers adapt, but whether the adapted behavior still benefits the protocol.

Note 06 Post-TGE

The snapshot is diagnostic, not corrective.

By the time eligibility is being debated on Twitter, the design flaw has already compounded. The work happens months earlier — in the rule set.
