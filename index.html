02 — Mental Models

How
I Think

These aren't frameworks copied from a playbook. They come from years of watching the same patterns play out across hundreds of campaigns — from the other side of the fence.

I was the user projects try to filter out. Now I help projects build systems that do the filtering.

Observation 01 · Farming
The problem isn't
bad actors — it's bad incentives.
Sybil attacks are rational behavior. When a protocol makes farming more profitable than genuine use, it selects for farmers. The fix isn't stricter KYC — it's aligning economic incentives with the behaviors you actually want to see.
Observation 02 · Retention
Airdrops create attention, not users.
Most projects measure campaign success at distribution day. Real retention is measured 90 days after TGE — when price pressure hits and the farmer exodus begins. Projects that survive this cliff had users before the airdrop, not because of it.
Observation 03 · Behavior
On-chain data tells you
what, not why.
Wallet behavior is a symptom. Understanding why users engage — or disengage — requires reading the social layer: Telegram discussions, Discord tone shifts, Twitter sentiment before on-chain patterns catch up. The signal lives in community dynamics first.
