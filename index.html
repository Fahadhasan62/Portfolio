Case Study 01

Why Most Airdrop Campaigns Fail to Retain Users

Over multiple years participating in airdrops and testnet campaigns, I observed a consistent pattern: most projects successfully attract attention, but fail to retain users after rewards are distributed.

Problem

Campaigns are designed to maximize participation volume, not user quality. Low-effort tasks attract multi-wallet farmers who exit immediately after rewards.

Observation

Users behave rationally. If rewards can be extracted without long-term engagement, most participants will choose that path.

Insight

The issue is not user behavior — it is incentive design.

Strategic Fix

Introduce layered participation, combine behavioral signals, delay rewards, and build identity before distribution.

Outcome

Better user quality and higher retention probability.

Case Study 02

How Airdrop Farmers Exploit Campaign Systems

Across multiple campaigns, a consistent pattern emerges: once a campaign structure is predictable, it becomes easy to scale exploitation using multiple wallets and automated workflows.

Problem

Many campaigns rely on simple, repeatable tasks such as social follows, basic transactions, or form submissions. These tasks are easy to replicate across dozens or hundreds of wallets.

Exploit Pattern

Farmers typically combine multiple wallets, VPN rotation, and scripted actions. Once a workflow is defined, it can be repeated at scale with minimal additional effort.

Weak Points

Campaigns become vulnerable when:
• Tasks require low effort and no sequencing
• There is no cross-verification between actions
• Rewards are distributed instantly without filtering
• Wallet history is not considered

Insight

Exploitation is not random — it follows structure. Once a system can be repeated, it will be scaled. The simpler the system, the easier it is to farm.

Strategic Fix

Reduce repeatability:
• Introduce task dependency and sequencing
• Mix on-chain and off-chain actions
• Require time-based participation
• Analyze wallet behavior instead of single actions

Outcome

Systems designed this way increase friction for farmers while remaining manageable for real users, improving overall campaign quality.

Case Study 03

Why Users Disappear After TGE

Many projects experience strong growth before token distribution, followed by a sharp drop in activity. This pattern is not random — it is a direct result of how incentives are structured.

Problem

Users join primarily for rewards, not for the product. When the reward event ends, there is no remaining reason to stay.

Observed Behavior

Activity peaks before snapshot or TGE, then declines rapidly. Communities lose engagement, Discord becomes inactive, and token holders disengage unless price speculation takes over.

Root Cause

Campaigns are designed as short-term extraction events. Users complete tasks, claim rewards, and exit because no long-term participation loop was created.

Insight

Retention cannot be added after TGE. If users are not connected to the product before rewards, they will not stay after.

Strategic Fix

Build pre-TGE retention loops:
• Introduce roles, identity, and progression systems
• Reward ongoing participation, not one-time actions
• Align incentives with product usage, not task completion
• Gradually distribute rewards instead of one-time drops

Outcome

Projects that design for retention early maintain stronger communities, more active users, and better long-term ecosystem health.
